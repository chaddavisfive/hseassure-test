Safety for Supervisors

Course Description

Good supervisors with proper training will be able to direct and lead employees to increase efficiency and improve their performance. The possible effects of supervisor training can range from a simple change in the quality of work to helping to lower costs. In today’s demanding business environment, a well-run, efficient, safe workplace is critical to ensuring projects are completed on time and on budget.

The fact is that better supervisors produce better results, so giving your supervisors the tools to effectively manage site operations and contractors is key to your business and your clients’ satisfaction. Supervisor training will help to get your management staff in the best possible shape and ready to lead your company. A strong management staff means your employees will be led and directed to be the best workers possible. Effective and efficient management is the main goal of supervisor training.

What is Taught at Supervisor Training

During training, attendees are taught a variety of skills and techniques to improve workplace safety, their own performance, and that of the employees they manage.

Who Will Benefit from Supervisor Training

Supervisor training has far-reaching benefits. When you improve the performance of supervisors, you are improving the performance of everyone in the company.

Course Outline

By the end of the course, the successful learner will understand:

  • The role and function of a supervisor
  • The basic principles of effective communication
  • The theories underlying an HSE Management System
  • Conducting a Risk Assessment
  • The main components of a HSE Management System
  • Common causes of incidents and basic prevention techniques
  • The legal requirements concerning the employer’s basic duties and responsibilities
  • Hazards and risks associated with a workplace
  • General principles of occupational health in the workplace, including the functions of OHS personnel
  • Environmental awareness and waste management
  • Basic understanding of fire management in the workplace
  • An overview of management responsibility

Formative Assessment

  • Various activities will be completed during class as well as for homework.

Summative Assessment – Theory

  • Written assessment to be done at the end of the course.
  • Learners must achieve a minimum of 65%.

Summative Assessment – Practical

  • Workplace Assessment
  • Height Safety
  • Handling and Storing Hazardous Chemical Substances
  • PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)
  • Lock Out Tag Out
  • Incident Investigation
  • Plan Task Observations

Course Information

Classroom Duration: 3 days
Prerequisites: Communication and Mathematical Literacy
Target Groups: Supervisors, HSE Practitioners
