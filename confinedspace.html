Confined Space Entry

Course Description

This course is designed to provide learners with the knowledge and skills required to work safely in confined spaces. Participants will understand the legal requirements, risks, and control measures associated with entry into confined or enclosed environments, and how to implement safe entry and rescue procedures.

Formative Assessment

  • Practical and theoretical exercises
  • Classroom discussions and scenario-based simulations

Summative Assessment

  • Written assessment and practical demonstration
  • Minimum pass mark of 65%

Course Outline

  • Definition and identification of confined spaces
  • Legislation and responsibilities
  • Hazards associated with confined spaces
  • Atmospheric testing and gas monitoring
  • Permit to work systems and entry permits
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safety gear
  • Emergency procedures and confined space rescue
  • Roles and responsibilities of the entry team
  • Safe use of equipment such as harnesses, tripods, and ventilation systems

Course Information

Duration: 1 Day
Pre-Requisites: Basic safety awareness
Target Groups:

  • Maintenance teams and plant operators
  • Supervisors and safety officers
  • Emergency response and rescue teams
  • Anyone required to enter or oversee confined space work
